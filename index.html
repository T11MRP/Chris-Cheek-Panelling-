The trade

A finish that’s measured, not bought off a shelf.

Panelling changes a room more than paint or paper ever will — it adds depth, shadow and a sense that the space was finished properly. Every job is set out to the wall it’s going on, scribed to skirting and sockets, and fitted to sit flush and square.

Whether it’s a single feature wall or panelling that runs through a whole hallway, the work is supply-and-fit: design, materials and installation handled start to finish, for homes and commercial spaces alike.

What we fit

Four ways to panel a wall.

Each style suits a different room and a different look. Not sure which fits your space? That’s part of the quote.

01

Traditional panelling

Classic framed wainscoting and full-height panels. Period-correct proportions for hallways, lounges and stairwells.

02

Slatted & fluted

Vertical slat walls in timber or painted finish. Warm, modern and tactile — a strong backdrop for beds and media walls.

03

Media walls

Panelling built around the television, with a framed recess so the screen sits flush as part of the wall rather than on top of it.

04

Bespoke feature walls

One-off geometric and battened designs, set out by hand for a single wall that nobody else has.

How we work

From bare wall to finished feature.

A straightforward way of working, so you know what’s happening at each stage.

01

Visit & quote

We come to the room, measure up, talk through styles and finishes, and put a clear free quote together. No guesswork off a photo.

02

Set out & build

The design is set out to your wall — centred, level and worked around sockets and skirting — then the panelling is cut and fixed in place.

03

Fill, finish & hand over

Joints filled, caulked and sanded ready for paint, or finished in your chosen colour. We leave the room clean and the wall ready to live with.

Finishes & materials

Know your panelling styles.

A quick guide to the four looks people ask for most — so the words mean the same thing to both of us before we start.

  • Shaker & wainscoting

    Flat panels inside a moulded frame. The traditional look — clean lines, classic proportions, sits well in older and newer homes alike.

  • Slatted

    Evenly spaced vertical battens, in natural timber or painted. Adds warmth, texture and a sense of height to a wall.

  • Fluted

    Tighter, rounded grooves for a softer, more refined ribbed surface — a subtler cousin of the slatted wall.

  • Geometric & bespoke

    Battens set out in a pattern — diagonals, chevrons, mixed angles. Designed for one wall, one room, one off.
